Transition Metals

Important Facts:

*Blue highlighted are the more important notes

  • Any compound containing a transition metal is a binary ionic compound (Look at the Ionic Molecules page)
  • For transition metals, you would use roman numerals to identify their charge.

Naming Binary Ionic Compounds

the transition metal present in the formula is first in the name of the whole compound and does not change.

e.g. Fe is Iron so, FeCl2 = Iron(II) Chloride


The second Element in the formula (always a nonmetal) is the second word in the compound's name and adds the -ide suffix

e.g. Cl is Chlorine so, FeCl2= Iron(II) Chloride

Optional Alternative

:

you can use specific names that clarify the charge of the element

  • Fe+2 = Ferrous
  • Fe+3 = Ferric
  • Sn+ = Stannous
  • Sn+2 = Stannic

General Rule: -ous has a lower charge than -ic

e.g. Fe+2 + Cl- = Iron(II) Chloride = Ferrous Cholride

Exceptions on Constant Charges of Some Transitions

:

All of these elements have constant charges and do not require a Roman Numeral.

  • Silver (Ag+1)
  • Zinc (Zn+2)
  • Scandium (Sc+3)
  • Cadmium (Cd+2)
  • Aluminum (Al+3)
  • Gallium (Ga+3)
