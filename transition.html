Transition Metals

Important Facts:

*Blue highlighted are the more important notes

  • Any compound containing a transition metal is a binary ionic compound

Naming Binary Ionic Compounds

the transition metal present in the formula is first in the name of the whole compound and does not change.

e.g. FeCl2 = Iron(II) Chloride


Transition metals' charge varies in value but they are all cations (always positive). Because of this we must clarify it's charge in the name of the compound. We do this by placing a roman numeral next to the transition metal.

e.g. Fe+2 + Cl- = Iron(II) Chloride

  • EXCEPTIONS: Silver, Zinc, Scandium, Cadmium, Aluminum, Gallium have constants charges and do not require a Roman Numeral.
  • Ag=+1 Zn=+2 Sc=+3 Cd=+2 Al=+3 Ga=+3
