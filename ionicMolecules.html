Ionic Molecules

Ionic (Binary) Molecules

Important facts:

*Blue highlighted are the more important notes

  • Forms between 2 ions from representative elements
  • Cations first, Anions second
  • End the name of the bond with -ide
  • Both elements should make a neutral molecule
  • Molecule should go together in smallest, whole # ratios

How to make Ionic Formulas

The Kamine Way:

You would first write down both molecules, Cation first, Anion second.

After that, you take notice of the charge of each molecule. For example, Ca and Cl.

The easiest way to make the formula is to switch the numbers. Take Ca's charge of 2 (IGNORE THE + & - SIGN), then criss-cross to move it bottom right. You get Cl2. Do the opposite, and you get Ca. (Just a negative sign means that it has a charge of 1)

Naming Formulas

All you have to do for this, is just get both of the names of the molecules and add an -ide on the end of the second molecule.

Example:

NaCl. Na = Sodium; Cl = Chlorine. Remove "ine" from chlorine, and add -ide. You get Sodium Chloride
